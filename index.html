Momentum based long-short strategy

Momentum trading is a strategy that involves buying assets that have shown strong recent performance (upward price movement) and selling those that have shown poor performance. The idea is that assets that are trending will continue in the same direction for some time due to market psychology and investor behavior. Traders typically use technical indicators like moving averages, RSI, or MACD to identify entry and exit points.

Portfolio Optimization and Efficient Frontier

Portfolio optimization is the process of selecting the best asset mix to maximize returns for a given level of risk (or minimize risk for a given return), based on historical data, forecasts, and constraints. It typically involves techniques like mean-variance analysis, using inputs such as expected returns, volatilities, and correlations among assets. The efficient frontier is a concept from Modern Portfolio Theory that represents the set of optimal portfolios offering the highest expected return for each level of risk. Portfolios on the frontier are considered well-diversified and efficient, balancing risk and return better than any other combinations.

Option pricing ( Black-Scholes and GARCH )

The Black-Scholes model is a mathematical formula used to estimate the theoretical price of European call and put options, based on variables like the underlying asset price, strike price, volatility, time to maturity, and risk-free interest rate. It assumes constant volatility and no arbitrage, providing a foundation for options valuation. GARCH models are used to forecast time-varying volatility in financial markets by modeling the clustering of volatility over time. They help improve risk assessment, asset pricing, and derivative valuation by accounting for changing variance rather than assuming it is constant.

High Frequency Trading

High-Frequency Trading is a form of algorithmic trading that uses ultra-low-latency systems and high-speed market data to execute thousands of trades within milliseconds. It leverages statistical models, quantitative strategies, and real-time analytics to capitalize on micro-price movements and market inefficiencies, often across multiple asset classes and exchanges.

Multi-factor Smart-Beta

Multi-Factor Smart Beta is an investment strategy that combines multiple systematic factors—such as value, momentum, quality, size, and low volatility—to construct diversified portfolios that aim to outperform traditional market-cap-weighted indices. It blends passive indexing with active factor exposure, using rules-based portfolio construction to enhance risk-adjusted returns.

Monte-Carlo simulation

Monte Carlo simulations are a statistical technique used to model the probability of different outcomes by running a large number of simulations using random variables. In finance, they are widely used for portfolio risk analysis, option pricing, and forecasting, helping assess uncertainty and variability in complex, dynamic systems.
